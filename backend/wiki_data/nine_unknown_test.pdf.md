# nine_unknown_test.pdf

In 273 BC, Emperor Ashoka of the Maurya Dynasty established a secret society in India known as the 
Nine Unknown Men. After the bloody Kalinga War, Ashoka entrusted this elite council with 
preserving and hiding dangerous ancient knowledge to prevent humanity from destroying itself. The 
society guarded nine distinct books of advanced science. The First Book contained the secrets of 
Propaganda and Psychological Warfare. Another member, an ascetic who operated out of hidden 
temple networks in Magadha, guarded the Second Book on Physiology, which detailed martial arts 
techniques including the 'touch of death'. Legend states that the knowledge from these books was 
never allowed to fall into the hands of foreign empires.
