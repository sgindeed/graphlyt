The city of NeoVastra was founded by Dr. Arvind Rao, a researcher from the Institute of Advanced
Systems, in collaboration with engineer Meera Sen and entrepreneur Karan Malhotra. Their
initiative, Project Synapse Grid, aimed to integrate artificial intelligence, urban infrastructure, and
human behavior analytics. Dr. Rao previously worked at QuantumCore Labs alongside Professor
Elena Markov, who developed the Markov Cognitive Engine, a system later acquired by TechNova
Corporation. TechNova partnered with GreenGrid Energy, led by CEO Ritu Sharma, to deploy
sustainable energy solutions across NeoVastra. Meanwhile, the Urban Mobility Division, headed by
Vikram Iyer, collaborated with AutoDyn Motors and SkyLift Aviation to introduce hybrid transport
systems. These systems used algorithms derived from the Synapse Grid and data collected from
citizens via the LifeTrack mobile application, developed by ByteForge Solutions. ByteForge
Solutions was founded by Neha Kapoor and Rohan Das, both alumni of the National Institute of
Technology. Their application integrated with HealthNet Services, a healthcare provider managed
by Dr. Sameer Khanna, enabling real-time health monitoring. Simultaneously, the Security
Intelligence Bureau, under director Ananya Bose, utilized surveillance data processed through the
Orion Analytics Platform, developed by DataSphere Inc. DataSphere had partnerships with
CloudAxis and NetSecure Global, ensuring secure data storage and encrypted communication.
NeoVastra also hosted the International AI Ethics Council, where members like Dr. Liu Wen and
Professor James Carter debated policies regulating artificial intelligence. Their guidelines
influenced legislation passed by the Global Technology Governance Forum.

The Synapse Grid expanded into neighboring regions such as AetherVille and Solaris Bay, where
local governments collaborated with NeoVastra’s Central Command Unit. The unit, led by
Commander Rajat Singh, coordinated with emergency services including FireCore Response Team
and MedAssist International. MedAssist International worked closely with PharmaLife Industries,
which developed smart drugs using BioLink Research Labs’ genetic data models. BioLink was
founded by Dr. Aisha Rahman and funded by VentureSphere Capital, whose partners included
Olivia Chen and Daniel Wright. In the education sector, NeoVastra University partnered with
LearnSphere Online and EduTech Innovations to provide adaptive learning systems. These
systems leveraged data from LifeTrack and integrated with CognitiveX AI, a startup incubated at
TechNova Corporation. CognitiveX AI collaborated with RoboDynamics Ltd. to deploy autonomous
assistants across public services. These assistants interacted with citizens and reported behavioral
insights back to the Synapse Grid. However, a cybersecurity incident involving NetSecure Global
exposed vulnerabilities in the Orion Analytics Platform. This led to an investigation by the Digital
Forensics Agency, headed by Inspector Kavya Nair, who uncovered links between rogue
developers and an underground group known as ShadowCircuit. ShadowCircuit had infiltrated
multiple systems, including CloudAxis servers and ByteForge applications. The incident prompted a
global response involving the International AI Ethics Council and the Global Technology
Governance Forum, resulting in stricter regulations and the evolution of Project Synapse Grid into a
more secure and transparent ecosystem.
